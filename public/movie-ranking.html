Bleach

Bleach follows the story of Ichigo Kurosaki, a high school student with the ability to see ghosts. His life takes a dramatic turn when he encounters Rukia Kuchiki, a Soul Reaper (a supernatural being who protects humans from evil spirits called Hollows). Rukia is injured in a battle with a Hollow and transfers her Soul Reaper powers to Ichigo so he can defeat it.

This act leaves Rukia powerless, and Ichigo is forced to take on the role of a Soul Reaper, battling Hollows and guiding lost souls to the Soul Society (the afterlife). As the story progresses, Ichigo gets drawn deeper into the world of Soul Reapers, facing powerful enemies and uncovering secrets about the Soul Society and his own past. He gains allies among his classmates (who also develop abilities) and within the Soul Society itself.

Solo Leveling

In a world where hunters with supernatural abilities battle monsters to protect humanity, Sung Jin-Woo is the weakest hunter of all. Constantly risking his life in low-rank dungeons, he's barely scraping by. One day, during a particularly dangerous raid, he's left for dead by his fellow hunters. Just as he's about to die, a mysterious System chooses him as its Player. This System grants him the ability to level up and become exponentially stronger, similar to a character in a video game. Initially, he can only see the System's quests and notifications, but as he completes them, he gains experience, improves his stats, and acquires new skills. He starts climbing the ranks, hiding his newfound power while navigating the complex world of hunters, guilds, and the looming threat of powerful monsters and interdimensional gates. As he grows stronger, he uncovers secrets about the System and the true nature of the world, eventually becoming humanity's greatest weapon against the monstrous threats. It's a story of incredible power progression, hidden identities, and the fight to protect humanity against overwhelming odds.
